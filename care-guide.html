Watering Guide

Signs of Overwatering

  • Yellow or brown leaves
  • Soggy soil
  • Fungus or mold growth

Signs of Underwatering

  • Crispy, brown leaf edges
  • Drooping leaves
  • Dry, compact soil

Pro Tip: Always check soil moisture before watering. Insert your finger about 1-2 inches into the soil - if it's dry, it's time to water.

Light Requirements

Light Levels

  • Bright Direct: 6+ hours of direct sunlight
  • Bright Indirect: Well-lit but no direct sun
  • Medium: Away from windows but still bright
  • Low: No direct sun, minimal natural light

Pro Tip: Most indoor plants prefer bright, indirect light. Direct sunlight can burn leaves.

Optimal Conditions

Humidity Tips

Pro Tip: Keep plants away from drafts, AC vents, and heating sources.

Seasonal Care Tips

Summer Care

  • Increase watering frequency
  • Protect from intense afternoon sun
  • Maintain higher humidity
  • Watch for pest activity

Winter Care

  • Reduce watering frequency
  • Move plants away from cold windows
  • Stop fertilizing
  • Maintain consistent temperature

Common Problems & Solutions

Yellow Leaves

Usually caused by overwatering or poor drainage. Ensure proper watering schedule and well-draining soil.

Brown Leaf Tips

Often due to low humidity or mineral buildup. Increase humidity and use filtered water.

Pest Infestation

Regularly inspect plants and treat with neem oil or insecticidal soap at first sign of pests.
